I used to put too much into one AI conversation.

Strategy. Drafting. Summarizing. Planning. Debugging. Content ideas. Household systems. Possibly plant identification.

A perfectly normal range of human concerns.

Eventually, I realized the issue was not the model.

It was the lack of role clarity.

Human teams have roles for a reason:

- Strategist
- Analyst
- Operator
- Reviewer
- Auditor
- Assistant

AI needs the same distinction.

Some AI functions should explore. Some should execute. Some should check. Some should preserve context. Some should challenge assumptions. Some should produce clean artifacts.

When every AI is "the assistant," the work gets blurry.

The future may not be one model to rule them all.

It is **role-based intelligence with clear jobs**.
