A lot of AI productivity advice seems to assume the goal is to become a more efficient toaster.

More output. More speed. More content. More emails. More dashboards. More lightly formatted urgency.

I am not interested.

The point of AI should not be to cram more sludge into the same calendar.

The point should be to create **margin**.

For better judgment. Better work. Better thinking. Better relationships. Better rest. Better stewardship.

Optimization is not bad.

But optimization without a human purpose becomes a polished treadmill.

After years of experimenting with AI, I care less about whether it can make me faster.

I care more about whether it gives me capacity back.

I do not want AI to create art and write while I shop for groceries and check email.

You should not either.
