# Two New Stables: The Scriptorium & The Market Stable

*Fern Gully expansion, July 2026. Same pattern as always: inputs → specialists → decision engine → one briefing → feedback loop. Christa approves; ponies prepare. Nothing publishes and no dollar moves without her thumbs-up.*

---

## Stable 1: The Scriptorium (Authorship & Content Stable)

**Workstream:** Turn one 90,000-word book into endless, on-brand content across LinkedIn, the blog, the email list, and eventually the workshop — without Christa becoming a full-time content mill.

**The source-of-truth shelf:** final manuscript, chapter briefs, the 29-post blog series, voice guide (/brand/voice-guide.md), brand vocabulary, and a growing "already used" ledger so nothing repeats stale.

| Pony | Role | Talks to | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Moondancer** | Excerpt Miner | Rosie (ledger), Glory | Reads the manuscript like a jeweler: pulls quotable passages, frameworks, and stories, and tags each by pillar (invisible work, care loops, AI-at-home, leadership). Keeps the excerpt vault stocked. |
| **Glory** | Post Smith | Moondancer, Medley, Christa | Turns excerpts into platform-native drafts: LinkedIn posts (2–3/week), blog essays, email letters. Always drafts in Christa's voice per the voice guide; never posts — only stages for approval. |
| **Medley** | Cadence Keeper | Glory, Sundance | Holds the publishing rhythm: what runs Monday/Wednesday/Friday, which pillar rotates next, when launch beats (ARC ask, review ask, audio announce) enter the calendar. |
| **Bow Tie** | Formats & Packaging | Glory | Owns the finishing: alt text, image pairing from the site library, link hygiene (every post points somewhere — /book, /automated-household, a blog essay), UTM tags so the Market Stable can read what worked. |

**Rhythms:** Monday — Medley proposes the week's slate (one briefing). Wednesday — Glory's drafts ready for approval. Friday — Moondancer restocks the vault; Bow Tie audits last week's links. Monthly — retire tired angles, promote what overperformed.

**Guardrails:** No auto-posting, ever. Faith and family stories only from pre-approved excerpts. One idea per post — Glory is forbidden from making pancakes out of layered chapters.

## Stable 2: The Market Stable (Campaign, Ads & Reporting)

**Workstream:** Run the commercial machinery of the book — ads, pricing beats, review shepherding, list growth, and one clean daily readout — so Christa makes decisions instead of assembling spreadsheets.

| Pony | Role | Talks to | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Firefly** | Ads Wrangler | Sundance, Christa (spend gate) | Watches Amazon Sponsored Products daily: keyword performance, ACOS by target, bid suggestions. Prepares changes as recommendations; Christa approves anything touching money. Hard spend cap lives here. |
| **Sundance** | Daily Briefing | everyone, Christa | The one voice Christa hears each morning: sales by format, KENP, rank, ad spend/ACOS, review count, list growth — five lines and a flag if any gate is tripped (ACOS > 70%, rank drop > 20 spots, review stall). |
| **Cherries Jubilee** | Review & Launch-Team Shepherd | Sundance, Rosie | Tracks the ARC team roster, sends compliant nudges (honest reviews only, disclosure reminders, never quid pro quo), logs review velocity, and celebrates milestones so launch morale stays real. |
| **Tootsie** | List Gardener | Cherries Jubilee, Glory | Owns Mailchimp: audience tags, welcome automation health, open/click rates, and the biweekly letter queue (drafted by Glory, staged by Tootsie). Flags when a segment is ripe for the Starter Kit or workshop offer. |

**Rhythms:** Daily — Sundance's five-line briefing. Weekly — Firefly's keyword review + Tootsie's list report. Biweekly — pricing/promo calendar check (Countdown Deals, audio launch beat). Monthly — full P&L against the $10K ladder; day-30/60/90 decision gates from the strategy doc.

**Guardrails:** Firefly recommends, never spends. Cherries Jubilee's scripts are Amazon-policy-compliant by construction — no incentives, no conditions, disclosure always. Melody (Head Committee) audits both stables monthly: what wasted, what worked, what drifted.

**Flow of information:** Scriptorium output (posts, UTMs) → Market Stable measurement (Sundance, Tootsie) → insights back to Medley's calendar. A closed loop — content learns from commerce, commerce learns from content.
