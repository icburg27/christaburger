## About section (approved draft)

I build governance systems for complex work.

My work sits where cybersecurity, AI adoption, risk, trust, leadership, household life, and human behavior collide. For the last two decades, I have helped organizations decide how data, systems, identities, vendors, controls, customers, and leadership decisions should be handled when the stakes are real.

AI has made that work more urgent, but it is not the whole story. Most organizations do not fail because they lack tools. They fail because they lack clear decision structures: who decides, what values guide the decision, what risk is acceptable, how accountability flows, and how the system changes when reality changes.

That is the work I love: turning ambiguity into operating models leaders can use. Not governance theater. Not a policy nobody reads. Practical structures for responsible AI adoption, cybersecurity trust, customer assurance, enterprise risk, and sustainable leadership.

I also teach and write about the human side of AI and leadership: how people, families, and communities can use these tools without losing judgment, presence, or care. My household AI systems are not theoretical. They are running in real life, which keeps my work grounded in the world people actually inhabit.

The through-line is simple: technology should serve human flourishing. Governance gives that conviction a working shape.
