# The Automated Household — Launch & Revenue Strategy

*Prepared July 3, 2026. Book: "The Automated Household: Stop Being the System" (~90K words, drafting in progress). Goals: 100 sales + 20 honest reviews in month 1; $10K/mo within a few months. Audible edition self-narrated to follow.*

---

## 1. The honest math first

| Format | Price | Net to you (verified July 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Kindle ebook | $6.99 | ~$4.75 (70% royalty band is $2.99–$9.99, minus ~$0.10 delivery fee) |
| Kindle launch week | $0.99 | ~$0.35 (35% tier — velocity play, not revenue) |
| Paperback (6×9, ~330pp) | $17.99 | ~$5.90 (60% × list − ~$4.85 print cost) |
| Kindle Unlimited full read | — | ~$2.00–2.25 (≈450–500 KENP × ~$0.0045) |
| Audible (new 2026 model) | — | 50% of engagement revenue if exclusive, 30% non-exclusive; per-listen value varies |

**Blended net per unit ≈ $4.50–5.00.** So book royalties alone at $10K/mo = ~2,000 units/mo = 65/day. That is a real number for a niche nonfiction title — possible, but not typical by month 3.

**The strategic truth: the book is the front door, not the house.** $10K/mo arrives reliably when the book feeds a ladder:

| Rung | Asset | Price | Realistic month-3 volume | Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Book (all formats) | $7–18 | 400–600 units | $2,000–3,000 |
| 2 | The Stable System Starter Kit (digital: templates + prompts + system builder, sold direct via Lemon Squeezy/Gumroad) | $29–49 | 60–100 | $2,000–4,000 |
| 3 | The Automated Household Workshop (live cohort or recorded course) | $149–197 | 15–25 | $2,500–5,000 |
| 4 | Speaking / corporate workshops (book as calling card) | $2,500–7,500 | 1–2 | gravy on top |

Rungs 2–3 are 80%+ margin and convert from the *email list the book builds*. This is the $10K/mo machine. The book's job is reach, authority, and list growth; the backend's job is revenue.

## 2. Distribution decision: KDP Select first 90 days

Your primary reader (overwhelmed household managers, self-help/home adjacent) skews Kindle Unlimited-heavy. Recommendation: **enroll in KDP Select for the first 90-day term.**

- KU full-reads pay ~$2+ each and count toward rank — rank drives organic discovery.
- Free Book Promotion days (up to 5) and Kindle Countdown Deals power the launch spike.
- Re-evaluate at day 90: if KU pages read < 20% of revenue, go wide (Kobo, Apple, Nook) and add direct ebook sales from the site.
- Paperback and audiobook are never exclusive — publish everywhere from day one.

**Audible:** self-narrate (your voice IS the brand — warm, funny, executive). Under the 2026 royalty model, take **exclusive at 50%** initially; your listeners are membership listeners. Opt into All-You-Can-Listen. Record after the manuscript locks; launch audio ~4–6 weeks after ebook to create a second launch moment.

## 3. Month 1: 100 sales + 20 reviews (the compliant way)

Amazon's review rules, current as of 2026: ARCs are allowed if the free copy is disclosed by the reviewer, no review is required, no positive rating is requested, and nothing is exchanged. No paid reviews, no swaps, no "5 stars please." Violations get reviews stripped and accounts flagged — as a governance person, you get to be smug about doing this correctly.

**The launch sequence:**

1. **T-minus 4 weeks — build the ARC team (target 60–80 readers).** Recruit from: your email list (the stable-system-updates signups), LinkedIn announcement post, the Pretty_Dials community, CVA/personal network, and a service like BookSirens (~$10/reader match, policy-compliant). Expect a 25–40% review rate → 20+ reviews.
2. **T-minus 2 weeks — Mailchimp launch automation live** (see §4). Landing page collecting "launch team" signups with the free sample chapter.
3. **Launch day — price at $0.99 for days 1–5.** Email list + LinkedIn + ARC team nudge. Goal: concentrated velocity to spike category rank (choose 2–3 winnable subcategories, e.g., Home Management, Time Management for the Home, AI & Semantics — verify at upload).
4. **Day 3–5 — ask.** One email + back-matter page: "Reviews are how invisible books become visible. If this helped, an honest review is the biggest gift you can give it." (Never conditioned, never incentivized.)
5. **Day 6 — price rises to $4.99; day 14 → $6.99.** Rising-price urgency is honest and effective.
6. **Weeks 2–4 — Amazon Sponsored Products ads on** (see §5), 2 posts/week on LinkedIn from the book (Authorship Stable output).

100 sales month 1 = your list (even 300 subscribers × 20% buy = 60) + launch pricing + ARC team + organic LinkedIn. Very reachable.

## 4. Email list: yes, emphatically — Mailchimp is the spine

The list is the only channel you own. Amazon won't give you buyer emails; Mailchimp is where second-book launches, workshop cohorts, and Starter Kit sales come from. Structure:

- **Lead magnet:** free sample chapter ("The Task Is Never the Task") + the AI Margin Map one-pager. Delivered by Mailchimp automation.
- **Forms:** the new /book page + existing /automated-household/downloads page both feed a "Book" audience tag in Mailchimp. (Site forms currently run through Netlify; embed your Mailchimp form action or connect via Zapier — 10-minute job, noted in the page template.)
- **Welcome automation (5 emails):** 1) chapter delivery + your story, 2) the invisible-work thesis, 3) the Stable System tour (links to site), 4) launch/buy ask, 5) review ask + what's coming.
- **Cadence after:** one good letter every 2 weeks. The blog series gives you 29 ready essays.
- **Targets:** 500 subscribers by launch, 1,500 by day 90. Every 1,000 engaged subscribers ≈ $1,500–3,000 per backend promotion.

## 5. Where the ad money goes (and doesn't)

- **Amazon Sponsored Products: primary.** Buyer intent is on-platform. Start $10–15/day, auto campaign + exact-match on: mental load, household management, AI for moms, invisible labor, fair play, home organization systems. Kill keywords with ACOS > 70% during launch (rank-building tolerance), tighten toward 40–50% by month 3. Expected month 3: ~$400–600 spend driving 150–250 sales with halo organics.
- **Google Ads: not for the book.** Sending cold traffic to an Amazon page you can't retarget loses the thread. Use Google/Meta ads *later, for the workshop/Starter Kit* where you own the checkout and the pixel.
- **Google SEO: yes, but it's the slow flywheel, already built.** The 29-post series + /automated-household section is the SEO asset; each post should keep linking to /book. Expect meaningful search traffic at month 4–6, not month 1.
- **LinkedIn organic: your unfair advantage.** You have executive credibility + a story algorithms love (CISO with pony agents). 2–3 posts/week from book material (Authorship Stable). This is free and will likely outsell the ads early.

## 6. Path to $10K/mo (90–120 day plan)

| Month | Focus | Revenue picture |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch: 100+ sales, 20 reviews, list to 500, ads learning | $500–900 (velocity pricing suppresses revenue — by design) |
| 2 | Price normalized, ads scaling, audio recording, Starter Kit built & soft-launched to list | $1,500–3,000 |
| 3 | Audible launch (second spike), Starter Kit public at $39, first workshop cohort announced | $4,000–7,000 |
| 4 | Workshop runs, ads optimized, list 2,000+, corporate/speaking inbound begins | $8,000–12,000 |

The book alone plateaus near $2–4K/mo without a massive ad budget. Book + Starter Kit + workshop + audio crosses $10K — and every rung is content you already have.

## 7. Metrics that matter (Campaign Stable's dashboard)

Daily: sales by format, KENP read, ad spend/ACOS, rank in chosen categories. Weekly: review count + velocity, list growth, top-of-funnel (site sessions → /book conversions). Monthly: blended net/unit, revenue by rung, cost per subscriber. Decision gates: day 30 (ads keep/kill), day 90 (Select renew vs wide), audio day 30 (AYCL performance).
