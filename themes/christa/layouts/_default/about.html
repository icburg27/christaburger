My Story

Wisdom at the intersection of technology and humanity

I work at a threshold moment — one of those rare points in history when everything is being reorganized around a new force. Artificial intelligence is reshaping how organizations think, decide, communicate, and operate. My work is to help leaders, teams, and communities navigate that change with intention, wisdom, and care.

My background spans AI strategy and consulting, public education, and governance — but what connects all of it is a conviction that technology questions are ultimately human questions. The most important decisions in the AI era aren't about what systems can do. They're about what we should do, how we want to live, and what we want to protect.

I bring that lens to every engagement — whether I'm working with an executive team designing an AI strategy, speaking to a community organization about what AI means for their members, or helping a board build governance structures that actually hold.

What I believe

The AI moment demands more than technical expertise. It demands wisdom — the kind that comes from asking hard questions, sitting with complexity, and keeping human flourishing at the center of every decision.

I believe organizations that navigate this well will be defined not by how fast they adopted AI, but by how thoughtfully they integrated it. And I believe that thinking — strategic, relational, values-driven — is exactly what's needed right now.
